About the Snow Leopard

The snow leopard (Panthera uncia) is a large cat native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. Known for its beautiful thick coat and elusive nature, it is perfectly adapted to its cold, rocky habitat. Snow leopards have a unique ability to blend into their environment, which has made them a symbol of the wild places of the world.

Once distributed across a vast area, the snow leopard's range has now become fragmented due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. The species is currently listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List, with fewer than 7,000 individuals estimated to remain in the wild.

  • Name: Panthera uncia
  • Family: Felidae
  • Total Population: 4,000 to 6,500 individuals
  • Kingdom: Central and South Asia
  • Habitat: Mountain ranges, rocky slopes, alpine meadows, and forests
  • National Parks: Hemis National Park, Jammu & Kashmir; Snow Leopard Conservancy
  • Status in the Wild: Vulnerable
