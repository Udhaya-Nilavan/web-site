Lion-Tailed Macaque

About the Lion-Tailed Macaque

The lion-tailed macaque, also known as the bruh, is an Old World monkey endemic to the Western Ghats of South India. This species is distinguished by its black fur and a distinctive silver-white mane surrounding its head, which gives it the appearance of a lion. The tail, with a tuft at the end, resembles a lion's tail, although the tuft is more pronounced in males than in females. The lion-tailed macaque is one of the smallest macaques, with a head-body length ranging from 42 to 61 cm and a weight of 2 to 10 kg.

This species is found only in specific areas of the Western Ghats, at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 feet. Due to habitat loss, the lion-tailed macaque is considered endangered, with a remaining population of only around 3,000 to 3,500 individuals.

  • Name: Macaca silenus
  • Family: Cercopithecidae
  • Total Population: 3,000 - 3,500 individuals
  • Kingdom: Indian Subcontinent
  • Habitat: Evergreen forests, called sholas, in the Western Ghats of Southwest India
  • National Parks: Kalakkadu Wildlife Sanctuary, Tamil Nadu; Mundanthuri Wildlife Sanctuary, Tamil Nadu
  • Status in the Wild: Endangered
