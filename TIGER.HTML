Royal Bengal Tiger

About the Royal Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) is a majestic big cat native to the Indian subcontinent. Once found throughout India, Bangladesh, Bhutan, and Nepal, it is now primarily located in India. Sadly, this species is endangered due to poaching and habitat destruction. By 2011, fewer than 2,500 Bengal tigers were estimated to be in the wild.

As of 2018, there are between 2,603 to 3,346 Bengal tigers in India alone. The population of Bengal tigers in other regions, such as Bangladesh (440 tigers), Nepal (163-253 tigers), and Bhutan (103 tigers), continues to be monitored. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure the survival of this iconic species.

  • Name: Panthera tigris tigris
  • Family: Felidae
  • Total Population: 2,000 - 2,500 individuals
  • Kingdom: Indian Subcontinent
  • Habitat: Dense thickets, grasslands, and tamarisk shrubs along river banks
  • National Parks: Kanha National Park, Bandavgarh National Park
  • Status in the Wild: Endangered
