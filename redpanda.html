About the Red Panda

The Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens) is a small mammal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, the Red Panda is not closely related to the Giant Panda, but it is part of its own family, *Ailuridae*. This endangered species is known for its reddish-brown fur, long bushy tail, and playful nature. Red Pandas are arboreal, spending much of their time in trees and foraging for bamboo, fruits, acorns, and berries.

Red Pandas are facing a decline in population due to habitat loss, poaching, and competition with the larger Giant Panda. They are listed as Endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) due to these threats. Conservation efforts are underway, particularly in protected areas like the Singalila National Park in India and the Mount Emei area in China.

  • Name: Ailurus fulgens
  • Family: Ailuridae
  • Total Population: Fewer than 10,000 individuals
  • Kingdom: Eastern Himalayas and southwestern China
  • Habitat: Temperate forests with bamboo and conifer trees
  • National Parks: Singalila National Park (India), Mount Emei (China)
  • Status in the Wild: Endangered
